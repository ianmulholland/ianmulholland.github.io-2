My Signature

I live between a tourist and purist approach: I love to approach things with a spirit of experimentation and newness, but value the lessons and learnings of those who have done work beforehand.

I value fostering relationships - and there's no better way to connect than through humor. The space to laugh is incredibly powerful - life's not that serious!

Having a mother as a nurse and a father that was in the Navy, service is encoded into everything I do. My work is guided by impact more than just dollar signs.

My Design Language

Before this year, I've never been to an IKEA. No, I'm not just inspired by their meatballs. I believe in democratic design, particularly it's first pillar: function. I work on projects that have a need in communities that they serve and are meant for.

What guides me

If I could invite 3 people to dinner, I'd invite Roberto Clemente, Virgil Abloh, and my grandfather. Clemente represents not only a Herculian type of figure, half mortal and half diety,on the baseball field, but also embodied and lived a life around impact and others. His focus on his community always reminds me to work towards supporting others. Virgil Abloh is my proverbial Virgil through the 9 circles of design. His simple and functional approach drives my work. And finally, no person has had a greater impact on me than my grandfather, who served in the Navy during WWII and as a Boston Fireman. To me, he embodied service with humor.

What guides me

Professionally, my goal is to use data to create inferential tools to amplify impact for grassroots and social sector organizations. I believe that there is a technical gap between the social sector and the private, and my goal is to close that gap. I've seen how impactful and important data can be for grassroots organizations in making decisions that benefit communities and I am working towards a Masters in Information Management Science to get to that goal.
