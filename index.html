A little more about me

I was 6 when I started collecting baseball cards. They’re stored in boxes, binders, display cases, and are used in less orthodox ways as coasters, bookmarks, and to balance uneven furniture legs around the house. The excitement of ripping a pack of cards open to find my favorite player has never waned, but I’ve become more drawn to the rich storytelling and deep insights that cards are able to tell on 3 inches of cardboard: the advanced statistics can tell the story of a single player, the photograph on the front can show you the facial hair trends of the era, and a collection of players can reveal a team’s success. Having worked in a grassroots NGO in India, a social impact initiative at MIT, and a mobile tech startup in Boston, I’ve personally used, witnessed, and leveraged data to strengthen impact in communities and draw actionable insights to lead growth. I am a student at UC Berkeley, studying how data science and ML can be applied to the impact measurement space. If you love to geek out on social impact, DS / ML, impact measurement, baseball, or memorabilia, drop me a line!
